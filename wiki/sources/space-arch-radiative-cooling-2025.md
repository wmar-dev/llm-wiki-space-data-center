# Radiative Cooling for Space Data Centers

**Source**: Space Architecture Research Group, 2025
**Source type**: industry_report
**Processing status**: processed
**Ingest count**: 2

## Summary

Space data centers cannot use convective cooling because there is no atmosphere to carry heat away. All waste heat must be rejected via thermal radiation. The source establishes two key figures:

- Radiators operating at ~300 K can reject approximately **450 W/m²**
- A **1 MW** data center therefore requires approximately **2,200 m²** of radiator surface area

These figures define a hard physical constraint on space data center design: radiator area scales linearly with power load.

## Key Claims

- Without convective cooling, heat must be radiated directly to space.
- Radiators at ~300 K → ~450 W/m² rejection capacity.
- 1 MW load → ~2,200 m² radiator surface required.
