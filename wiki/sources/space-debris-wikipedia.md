## Summary

Comprehensive overview of space debris: sources, characterization, hazards, tracking, and mitigation.

## Key Claims

- ESA tracks 40,230 artificial objects in orbit as of April 2025. — *(other)*
- Estimated 128M pieces <1 cm, 900K pieces 1–10 cm, 34,000+ >10 cm. — *(other)*
- 8,000 metric tons of debris in orbit as of 2020. — *(other)*
- 85% pollution in LEO. — *(other)*
- Average LEO impact speed: 10 km/s, max >14 km/s. — *(other)*
- 2009 satellite collision (Iridium 33 + Kosmos 2251): 11.7 km/s closing speed, 2,000+ large fragments. — *(other)*
- 2011 US National Research Council: debris at "tipping point" — enough in orbit to continually collide and create more debris. — *(other)*
- 2006 NASA model: even with no new launches, environment would self-generate by ~2055. — *(other)*
- Space debris is a negative externality — costs not borne by launcher. — *(other)*
- ITU now requires end-of-life disposal plans. — *(other)*

## Domain Relevance

Establishes the current state and trajectory of orbital debris, which constrains the number of objects (including data centers) that can safely operate in orbit.
