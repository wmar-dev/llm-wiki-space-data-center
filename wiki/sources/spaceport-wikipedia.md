# Spaceport (Wikipedia)

**Source**: Wikipedia
**Source type**: other
**Processing status**: processed
**Domain relevance**: supporting (launch site infrastructure relevant to space data center deployment constraints)

## Key Claims

- **Countries with operational orbital launch sites**: Russia/Kazakhstan (Baikonur, Plesetsk, Vostochny), US (Cape Canaveral, Kennedy, Vandenberg), China (Jiuquan, Taiyuan, Wenchang, Xichang), India (Satish Dhawan), Japan (Tanegashima, Uchinoura), France (Guiana Space Centre), and others (Iran, Israel, South Korea, North Korea, New Zealand, Australia). — *(other)*
- **US launch congestion (January 2025)**: "Traffic congestion was reported at U.S. rocket-launch sites due to the rising number of launches" from SpaceX and Blue Origin; three sites in Florida and California handle the majority of US launches. — *(other)*
- **SpaceX**: Operates Starbase (Boca Chica, Texas) in addition to Cape Canaveral/Kennedy pads. — *(other)*
- **Planned new sites**: Proposals in Canada, India, Indonesia, Japan, Sweden, UK, and the US, with minimal published timelines. — *(other)*

## Significance

The January 2025 congestion event shows that launch site throughput is already a
real constraint at current (pre-Starship, pre-space-data-center) launch rates. A
gigawatt-scale orbital data center buildout would require a step-change in either
launch site capacity or launch cadence — or both.
