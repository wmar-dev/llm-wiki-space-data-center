# Unseen Cost of Space Computing: Quantifying LEO Battery Aging via Physics-Driven Modeling

**Authors**: Li Zeng, Jingyang Zhu, Zixin Wang, Yuanming Shi, Khaled B. Letaief
**Submitted**: March 4, 2026
**Source type**: peer_reviewed (arxiv preprint)
**Origin URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04372

## Summary

First paper to establish a physics-informed model that directly links computational workload execution on LEO satellites to physical degradation of onboard batteries. Challenges the adequacy of existing Depth of Discharge metrics for quantifying the true cost of compute workloads on battery lifetime.

## Key Claims

- Intensive computation in Space Computing Power Networks incurs an "unseen cost" through accelerated battery degradation from repeated eclipse charge-discharge cycles
- Existing DoD metrics fail to capture the nonlinear, workload-dependent nature of battery aging
- A physics-driven model can quantitatively link computational task parameters to nonlinear battery degradation — claimed to be the first such model
- Optimal strategy: prioritize immediate solar energy use under abundant solar conditions; use reactive real-time battery-state monitoring under energy-scarce conditions

## Significance

Directly relevant to orbital data center power management: compute scheduling decisions have non-obvious physical effects on battery lifetime that compound over the facility's operational life.
