# Space Data Center (Wikipedia)

**Source**: Wikipedia
**Source type**: other
**Processing status**: processed
**Domain relevance**: primary (comprehensive landscape of companies and projects)

## Summary

Wikipedia's dedicated article on space data centers. Most comprehensive single-source
listing of active companies and projects as of mid-2026.

## Key Claims

- **Starcloud**: Y Combinator-backed; deployed NVIDIA H100 in November 2025; claims to be "first company to train an LLM in space"; plans 88,000-satellite constellation; announced Bitcoin mining plans. — *(other)*
- **SpaceX**: Filed FCC plans for millions of satellites in January 2026 for space data center use (Starlink integration). — *(other)*
- **Blue Origin**: Unveiled TeraWave constellation (~5,400 satellites) for "high-throughput networking." — *(other)*
- **Google**: Project Suncatcher; feasibility study published November 2025; suggests cost-effectiveness achievable by ~2035. — *(other)*
- **Edge Aerospace**: Selected by ESA under Space Cloud program (May 2026) for "orbital data center" study. — *(other)*
- **Lonestar**: Deployed data backup hardware on the lunar surface (March 2025). — *(other)*
- **Cowboy Space Corporation**: Formerly Aetherflux; same company, renamed. — *(other)*
- **Nvidia**: Providing H100 processors for space deployment. — *(other)*
- **OpenAI**: Referenced as having interest in space-based infrastructure (no details). — *(other)*
- **University of Pennsylvania**: Presented tether-based architecture at AIAA SciTech (January 2026). — *(other)*

## Credibility Note

Wikipedia article; individual company claims should be cross-checked with primary
sources. Funding, satellite specifications, and launch dates are not confirmed for
most entities listed. Treat as a discovery/landscape source, not a technical reference.
