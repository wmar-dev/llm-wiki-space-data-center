# Commercial Laser ISL Terminals for LEO — Market Survey

**Source**: Satsearch Blog, May 1, 2025
**Source type**: industry_report (supply chain overview)
*(industry report)*

## Summary

Survey of 15 commercially available optical inter-satellite link (OISL) terminals as of May 2025. Covers vendors, data rates (where disclosed), form factors, and applicable orbits. Laser ISL avoids ITU/FCC frequency coordination and offers LPI/LPD properties attractive for defense.

## Key Products and Data Rates

| Vendor | Product | Data Rate | Form Factor |
|---|---|---|---|
| CACI | CICADA™ Ultra-Lite | 150 Mbps @ 2,000 km; 75 Mbps @ 4,000 km | Low SWaP |
| BAE Systems | Space-based laser | Multi-Gbps | Space-ground + ISL |
| Hanwha | LEO ISL | 1 Gbps (1.4 km test) | Commercial |
| Tesat | SCOT20 | Not published | 1U, 1.6 kg, LEO-ground + ISL |
| Tesat | SCOT80 | Not published | Large constellations, 8,000 km |
| Mynaric | CONDOR Mk3 | Not published | 7+ yr LEO lifetime |
| Voyager | µLCT™ | 400+ Gbps | Full duplex, GEO-GEO 80,000 km |
| SpaceX | Plug and Plaser | Not published | Uses Starlink relay network |

## Key Observations

- Most vendors do not publish sustained data rates publicly
- CACI CICADA is the low-end commercial benchmark: 150 Mbps at 2,000 km
- Voyager µLCT claims 400+ Gbps — highest public commercial claim
- Tesat is the heritage ISL supplier (used in Starlink constellation)
- Laser ISL does not require spectrum coordination (unlike RF), reducing regulatory burden
- Products serve ISL (sat-to-sat) and ground downlink; not all support both modes
