## Summary

This article provides a detailed cost breakdown for SpaceX Starship, tracking how per-kilogram launch costs descend from $150/kg (expendable) to $10/kg (100 reuses) through full reusability, engine mass production, and economies of scale.

## Key Claims

- Starship build cost trajectory: $90M (2024) → $50M (2025) → $20M (2027) → $2–5M (future mass production). — *(blog post)*
- Raptor engines are ~$1M each currently (39 engines per stack = $39M); mass production target is $250K each ($10M per stack). — *(blog post)*
- Stainless steel construction: ~$5/kg, ~$1.5M for the raw steel of a full Super Heavy + Starship stack. — *(blog post)*
- Cost per kg at reuse milestones: $150/kg (expendable), $90/kg (5 flights), $25/kg (10 flights), $10/kg (100 flights). — *(blog post)*
- Falcon 9 cannot go below ~30–40% of build cost per flight because the upper stage is expended. — *(blog post)*
- Full reuse of both stages (not just the booster) is the key enabler for the 10× cost reduction beyond Falcon 9. — *(blog post)*
- Future expendable Starship (300 t capacity, $2M build + $2M ops): ~$15/kg even without reuse. — *(blog post)*
- SpaceX Raptor factory targets 4,000 engines/year capacity. — *(blog post)*

## Cost Projections Table

| Scenario | Build Cost | Reuses | Cost per Flight | Payload | $/kg |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Expendable (2024) | $90M | 1 | $90M | ~200 t | ~$450 |
| 5 reuses (2024-25) | $90M | 5 | $28M | ~200 t | ~$90 |
| 10 reuses (2025-26) | $50M | 10 | $12M | ~200 t | ~$25 |
| 100 reuses (2027-28) | $20M | 100 | $2.2M | ~220 t | ~$10 |
| Future expendable | $2M† | 1 | $4M | ~300 t | ~$15 |

†**Future expendable caveat**: The $2M build cost is the most speculative figure in
this table. It assumes Starship reaches mature mass production at high volume — a
curve that has not yet been validated. The logic: at full production, Raptor engines
drop to ~$250K each ($10M for 39), raw stainless steel is already ~$1.5M per stack,
and remaining assembly costs fall to ~$0.5M. The expendable variant also gains ~300 t
payload (vs ~200 t reusable) by eliminating landing legs, grid fins, heat shield, and
return propellant reserve — which is why $/kg still falls to ~$15 even without reuse.
This scenario is a theoretical floor, not a near-term projection.
