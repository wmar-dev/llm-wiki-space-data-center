## Summary

Space-based solar power (SBSP) is the concept of collecting solar energy in orbit and transmitting it to Earth via microwave or laser. Although nominally about power generation, the article is directly relevant to space data center analysis because:

1. **Shared physical constraints**: thermal radiators, launch mass budgets, orbital mechanics, radiation hardening
2. **Aetherflux pivot**: In December 2025, Aetherflux (a $50M-funded SBSP startup) explicitly pivoted to space-based data centers
3. **Launch cost benchmarks**: $100–200/kg to GEO identified as the target for economic viability

## Key Claims

- Thermal waste heat disposal is "intractable" when spacecraft absorb maximum solar radiation. — *(other)*
- Launch cost to GEO must reach $100–200/kg for SBSP economic viability; current Falcon Heavy rate ~$2,000/kg. — *(other)*
- PV panels degrade ~8× faster in space than on Earth. — *(other)*
- Aetherflux ($50M funded) pivoted from SBSP to space-based data centers in December 2025. — *(other)*
- Active national SBSP programs: US, Japan, China, Russia, India, UK, ESA (as of 2020). — *(other)*
- Caltech MAPLE (2023): first successful wireless space power beaming to Earth (3.2W EIRP). — *(other)*

## Key Organizations

- **Aetherflux** — LEO laser SBSP constellation; pivoted to space data centers Dec 2025 [[wiki/entities/aetherflux.md]]
- **Caltech MAPLE** — Demonstrated wireless power beaming to Earth (2023)
- **JAXA** — 1.8 kW beamed wirelessly 50m (2015); long-term national SBSP goal
- **China CAST** — 200-tonne megawatt-grade SBSP station planned by 2035
