# Global Data Center Capacity and Growth (Wikipedia)

**Source**: Wikipedia — Data center article
**Source type**: other
**Processing status**: processed
**Domain relevance**: supporting (terrestrial baseline for space data center scale comparison)

## Key Claims

**Current scale (2024)**:
- **1,136 hyperscale data centers** globally by end of 2024 — doubled over the previous five years. — *(other)*
- **5,381 data centers** in the US as of March 2024. — *(other)*
- **4,799 colocation centers** in 127 countries as of 2024. — *(other)*
- Top 3 operators (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud) account for **~59% of all hyperscale capacity globally**. — *(other)*

**Power demand**:
- US data center demand: **17 GW in 2022**, projected to reach **35 GW by 2030**. — *(other)*
- Global consumption: **415–620 TWh annually** (figures vary by source; ~1.5% of global electricity). — *(other)*
- IEA projects consumption could **double by 2030** to ~945 TWh. — *(other)*
- US share: **4.6–9.1% of US electricity generation by 2030**. — *(other)*

**AI-driven demand**:
- AI data centers can consume as much electricity as **100,000 households per 100 MW**. — *(other)*
- Water consumption: ~2,000,000 litres/day per 100 MW facility. — *(other)*

## Significance for Space Data Center Analysis

US demand alone reaches 35 GW by 2030 — nearly 7× Starcloud's entire 5 GW orbital
target for 2035. Even the most ambitious orbital buildout represents a rounding error
on the terrestrial growth trajectory.
