# On-Orbit Satellite Servicing

Source: Wikipedia (peer-reviewed / reference)

## Summary

On-orbit satellite servicing (OOS) refers to refueling, boosting, or repairing space satellites while in orbit. New commercial interest is driven by decreased launch costs and the rise of LEO constellations.

## Key Historical Missions

- **1984**: First orbital repair — Solar Maximum Mission (SMM) by Shuttle astronauts van Hoften and Nelson
- **1993–2009**: Five Hubble Space Telescope servicing missions (STS-61, STS-82, STS-103, STS-109, STS-125) — manual subsystem replacement
- **2007**: DARPA Orbital Express — first autonomous satellite servicing demonstration; ASTRO servicing satellite and NEXTSat modular satellite
- **2012**: DARPA Phoenix — aim to recycle retired satellite parts (cancelled 2015)
- **2019**: Northrop Grumman MEV-1 — extended Intelsat 901 lifetime by 5 years
- **2022**: Lockheed Martin MAP interface standard for on-orbit docking

## Implications for Orbital Data Centers

OOS is technically mature (demonstrated by Orbital Express, MEV, Hubble servicing). The Aerospace Corporation notes that satellites designed for OOS could upgrade their hardware every few years. Starship's large payload bay (9m diameter) and on-orbit refueling capability provide the logistics backbone for visiting-vehicle servicing. However, no mission has yet demonstrated GPU or compute-module replacement on orbit — the logistics and cost remain unvalidated for orbital data center hardware refresh.
