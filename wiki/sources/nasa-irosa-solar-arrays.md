# iROSA — Integrated Roll-Out Solar Arrays

**Source**: NASA Space Technology Mission Directorate (STMD)
**Source type**: peer_reviewed (NASA primary)
**Origin URL**: https://www.nasa.gov/directorates/stmd/impact-story-roll-out-solar-arrays/

## Summary

Documents the iROSA (Integrated Roll-Out Solar Array) technology, the current state of the art in large-area deployable orbital solar arrays. Provides authoritative power output figures and ISS deployment results.

## Key Claims

- Each iROSA produces **in excess of 28 kW** at beginning of life; ROSA technology capable of **>30 kW per panel**
- Six iROSAs installed on ISS → total station power **>250 kW** (>30% increase over prior capacity)
- Legacy ISS arrays continue producing ~95 kW combined
- Two additional iROSAs requested in 2023 would add ~15 kW
- Autonomous self-deployment using composite boom strain energy — **no motors required**
- Each iROSA is nearly half the size of the legacy ISS arrays
- Also used: Gateway Power and Propulsion Element (60 kW from two ROSAs), DART, commercial GEO satellites

## Significance

iROSA is the current large-area deployable solar array state of the art. The 250 kW total ISS figure illustrates the scale gap: a 1 MW orbital data center would require 4× the entire ISS power system in solar arrays alone. The motorless boom deployment is a key reliability advantage.
