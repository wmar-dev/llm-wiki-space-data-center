# Space Data Center Component Lifetime Analysis

**Source**: Aggregated from MDPI (2025), JAXA, NASA VES16 data, Aerospace Corporation, and academic sources
**Source type**: other
**Processing status**: processed
**Ingest count**: 1

## Summary

Aggregated data on the lifetime of key satellite components in LEO and GEO environments, relevant to space data center design.

## Key Claims

- Triple-junction (GaInP/GaAs/Ge) solar cells retain >85% initial efficiency after 1 year in LEO; Si cells degrade 25-35% in 6 months. — *(other)*
- Li-ion space batteries achieve >30,000 cycles (LEO) and >1,800 cycles (GEO), corresponding to 5-20 year mission lives depending on orbit. — *(other)*
- LEO satellites experience ~5,000 battery cycles per year (30 min eclipse per 90 min orbit). — *(other)*
- PV panels degrade ~8× faster in space than on Earth [[wiki/sources/space-based-solar-power.md]]. — *(other)*
- ~87% of US military/civil satellites and ~75% of commercial satellites meet or exceed their design life. — *(other)*
- Typical LEO satellite design life: 5-8 years; typical GEO: 15-18 years. — *(other)*
- Commercial semiconductor devices are designed for 3-7 year useful life; space-grade electronics last >15 years. — *(other)*
- Radiation (total ionizing dose, displacement damage) is the primary electronics lifetime constraint in LEO. — *(other)*
