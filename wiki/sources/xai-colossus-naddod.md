# xAI Colossus — NADDOD Analysis

**Source**: NADDOD (industry analysis blog)
**Source type**: industry_report
**Processing status**: processed

## Summary

Detailed analysis of xAI's Colossus supercomputer in Memphis, Tennessee, covering its phased expansion, GPU model mix, and power infrastructure.

## Key Claims

- **Phase 1 (initial)**: 100,000 NVIDIA H100 GPUs — *(industry report)*
- **Phase 1B (expansion)**: Expanded to ~450,000+ GPUs by December 2025, including H200s — *(industry report)*
- **Phase 2 target**: ~1 million GPUs with advanced cooling and power infrastructure — *(industry report)*
- **Power (current)**: ~200 MW peak — *(industry report)*
- **Power (Phase 2)**: Targeting ~1 GW with GB200 NVL72 racks and gas turbine expansion — *(industry report)*
- **Build timeline**: ~122 days to initial deployment — *(industry report)*
- **Location**: Repurposed furniture factory in Memphis, Tennessee — *(industry report)*
- **Developer**: CyrusOne (elite data center developer) — *(industry report)*
- **Infrastructure**: 15-mile fiber loop; gas turbines for power — *(industry report)*
- **GPU model mix**: H100s in Phase 1, H200s added in Phase 1B, GB200 NVL72 racks planned for Phase 2 (72 GPUs per rack) — *(industry report)*

## Credibility Note

NADDOD is an industry analysis blog. GPU count and model mix figures are consistent with other reporting but have not been officially confirmed by xAI. The distinction between "million GPU" targets and actual installed base may include announced but not yet deployed hardware.
