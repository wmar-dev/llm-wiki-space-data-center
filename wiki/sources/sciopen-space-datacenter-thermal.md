# Thermal Management Technologies for Space Data Centers: Current Status and Prospects

**Authors**: Jiahao Shi, Xudong Zhang, Min Yang, Jing Liu
**Published**: 2025
**Source type**: peer_reviewed (Chinese Journal of Space Science, SciOpen)
**DOI**: 10.12465/issn.0253-4339.20251030004
**Origin URL**: https://www.sciopen.com/article/10.12465/issn.0253-4339.20251030004

## Summary

Peer-reviewed survey of thermal management approaches for space data centers. Identifies the current validated power level ceiling and the three engineering constraints that intensify as power scales toward data-center requirements.

## Key Claims

- Current space thermal control systems are validated at power levels **on the order of several tens of kilowatts** — far below commercial data center requirements
- Scaling to hundreds of kW or MW intensifies three constraints:
  1. **Radiative heat rejection capacity** — inherent limit of the space environment
  2. **System mass growth** — passive and active component accumulation
  3. **Controllability** — under variable operating conditions
- Current baseline: combined passive (heat pipes, radiators, phase-change materials) and active (mechanically pumped fluid loops, two-phase convection) systems
- Emerging solutions: liquid metal cooling, microgravity two-phase heat transfer, compact thermal energy storage, lightweight radiators with controllable emissivity

## Significance

The only peer-reviewed source in the wiki that directly addresses space data center thermal management as a distinct field. The "tens of kW" validated ceiling is the key quantitative gap — a 1 MW facility requires 20–100× more thermal capacity than any currently flight-qualified system.
