# Caltech SSPP — First In-Space Mission: Successes and Lessons

**Source**: Caltech News
**Source type**: peer_reviewed (primary institutional)
**Mission dates**: January 3 – November 11, 2023
**Origin URL**: https://www.caltech.edu/about/news/space-solar-power-project-ends-first-in-space-mission-with-successes-and-lessons

## Summary

Documents the Caltech Space Solar Power Project's first orbital mission, carrying three experiment modules: DOLCE (deployable structure), ALBA (solar cells), and MAPLE (wireless power transmission). Provides the most detailed published account of deployable structure failure modes in orbit.

## Key Claims

**DOLCE deployment failures**:
- Wire-snagging damaged a boom-to-structure connection during deployment — failure mode never observed in laboratory testing
- A secondary jam occurred after boom deployment — also never seen in lab
- Both resolved via workarounds: solar heating for the snag, vibration actuation for the jam
- Sergio Pellegrino: "The space test has demonstrated the robustness of the basic concept...new insights about ultralight deployable structure design"

**ALBA solar cells**:
- Current commercial space-grade solar cells cost approximately **100× more than terrestrial equivalents**
- Low-cost gallium arsenide cells performed consistently well
- Perovskite cells showed "tremendous variability" — not yet ready for orbital deployment
- 240+ days of operational data collected

**MAPLE wireless power**:
- Successfully demonstrated space-to-Earth wireless power beaming (June 2023)
- Late-mission degradation observed: individual transmitter element degradation and thermal-electrical interactions

## Significance

The DOLCE experience is the most important finding: two distinct deployment failure modes appeared only in orbit, not in ground testing. For MW-scale space data centers requiring thousands of m² of deployed structure, this failure-mode unpredictability is a fundamental risk that does not scale down from lab results.
