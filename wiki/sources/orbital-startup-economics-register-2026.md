# Orbital Datacenter Startup Economics

**Source**: The Register
**Published**: April 15, 2026
**Source type**: news_article
**Origin URL**: https://www.theregister.com/2026/04/15/orbital_space_datacenter_startup_economics/

## Summary

Profiles Orbital, an orbital data center startup funded by a16z, including CEO quotes on launch economics, satellite specifications, and industry skepticism. Most recent published primary source on the commercial orbital data center space.

## Key Claims

**Company**: Orbital; CEO Euwyn Poon (formerly Spin scooters); funded by a16z speedrun program

**Launch economics**:
- Current cost: ~$7,000/kg
- Target required: ~$10/kg ("a couple orders of magnitude" reduction)
- CEO: "as of today, the economics of launch don't quite work yet"

**Satellite specifications**:
- 100 kW per satellite — Poon: "really the sweet spot"
- Solar array: ~two halves of a tennis court
- Radiator: ~half the solar array area
- Planned chip: Nvidia Space-1 Vera Rubin module
- First PoC flight: targeting 2027; full-scale satellite: 2030

**Skepticism**:
- Gartner analyst Bill Ray: "peak insanity" — won't serve Earth applications "for decades, if ever"

## Significance

The CEO's explicit acknowledgment that launch economics are not yet viable — from someone building an orbital data center company — is the most candid public admission in the space. The 100 kW "sweet spot" is the most specific published constraint on practical near-term scale.
