# Aetherflux

**Type**: Company (venture-funded)
**Founded**: Unknown
**Funding**: $50 million
**Sector**: Space infrastructure

## Overview

Aetherflux is a venture-funded startup that originally proposed a constellation of small Low Earth Orbit satellites using infrared lasers to beam solar power to Earth (ground stations 5–10 meters in diameter). The company received support from the US Department of Defense's Operational Energy Capability Improvement Fund.

In December 2025, Aetherflux pivoted from space-based solar power to **space-based data centers**. — [[wiki/sources/space-based-solar-power.md]] *(other)*

## Significance

Aetherflux's pivot is notable as an early commercial signal that LEO laser infrastructure originally developed for SBSP is being repurposed for data center applications. The underlying LEO constellation architecture — small satellites, infrared laser links, small ground stations — may be directly applicable to orbital compute and storage.
