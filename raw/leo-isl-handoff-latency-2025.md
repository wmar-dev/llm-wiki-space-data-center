# Inter-Satellite Link Latency in LEO Constellations

Source: Various academic papers (2025-2026)
Type: Research synthesis
Date retrieved: 2026-06-07

Key findings:
- LEO ISL research shows end-to-end delay dominated by propagation delay (~5-25 ms at 550 km)
- ISL-based routing achieves 20-30 ms total latency (comparable to fiber)
- Handoff between ground stations typically adds <10 ms penalty
- Inter-plane ISL optimization reduces maximum delay by 3-9 ms (ENSGA-II algorithm)
- Laser-based ISLs offer >1 Gbps with low susceptibility to interference
- Starlink achieves ~45 ms RTT at 550 km (measured); ISL-enabled reduces ground station dependency
- Spare-factor design: "Frequent hand-off of User signals to a visible satellite increases cost & data processing complexity"
