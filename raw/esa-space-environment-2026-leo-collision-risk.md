# ESA Space Environment Report 2026: LEO Debris Collision Risk Up 20%

**Source**: FODNews coverage of ESA Space Environment Report 2025
**Published**: April 7, 2026 on FODNews
**Origin URL**: https://fodnews.com/esa-space-environment-report-2026-leo-debris-collision-risk/

## Key Statistics

- **20% increase in collision risk** between 2024 and 2026 (ESA 2026 Space Environment Report)

## Debris Population (ESA MASTER-8 model)

| Category | Count |
|----------|-------|
| Tracked objects | 40,000+ |
| Objects >10 cm (some untracked) | ~54,000 |
| Fragments 1–10 cm ("lethal non-trackable") | ~1,200,000 |
| Fragments <1 cm | ~140,000,000 |

## The 550-km Focal Point (Starlink Belt)

- Debris density at 550 km now approaches parity with active satellite density
- Starlink performed approximately **300,000 collision avoidance maneuvers in 2025**
  (50% increase from 2024)
- Projections: maneuvers could reach **1 million annually by 2027**
- Loss of maneuvering capability → catastrophic collision within **2.8–5.5 days**

## Economic Impact

World Economic Forum estimates **$25.8–$42.3 billion in costs** (2025–2035):
- Service disruptions: $14.7–$26.3B
- Physical asset losses: $10.5–$15.5B

## Long-Term Projection

ESA's DELTA modeling projects **debris density will double by 2030** under baseline
assumptions.
