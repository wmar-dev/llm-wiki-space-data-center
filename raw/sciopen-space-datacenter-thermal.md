# Thermal Management Technologies for Space Data Centers: Current Status and Prospects

**Authors**: Jiahao Shi, Xudong Zhang, Min Yang, Jing Liu
**Source**: SciOpen / Chinese Journal of Space Science
**DOI**: 10.12465/issn.0253-4339.20251030004
**Published**: 2025
**URL**: https://www.sciopen.com/article/10.12465/issn.0253-4339.20251030004

## Abstract

Addresses thermal constraints in space-based computing infrastructure. Argues that thermal management is "fundamentally constrained by thermal management technology" and that effective heat dissipation determines system reliability and scalability.

## Key Claims

**Current validated power levels**: Systems with power levels on the order of several tens of kilowatts using current approaches (passive + active thermal control)

**Current approaches**:
- Passive: heat pipes, thermal interface materials, phase change materials, radiators, coatings
- Active: mechanically pumped fluid loops and two-phase convection systems
- Combined passive-active strategies form the current baseline

**Three critical scaling constraints at higher power levels**:
1. Radiative heat rejection limits — inherent to space environment physics
2. System mass growth — passive/active component accumulation
3. Controllability challenges — under variable operating conditions

**Future technologies identified**: liquid metal cooling, microgravity two-phase heat transfer advances, compact thermal energy storage, lightweight radiators with controllable emissivity

## Significance

Peer-reviewed confirmation that current space thermal control is validated only to tens of kW — far below MW-scale data center requirements. The three scaling constraints provide the most rigorous published framing of the thermal management gap.
