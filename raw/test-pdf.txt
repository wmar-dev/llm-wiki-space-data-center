Space Data Center Power Budget Analysis
Published by: Orbital Systems Research Institute, 2025.
This report analyzes power budget constraints for orbital data centers.
Space-based computing facilities face unique thermal and power challenges.
Without atmospheric convection all waste heat must be radiated to space.
Radiative cooling sets a hard limit on power density per unit surface area.
Solar panels in LEO receive approximately 1.4 kilowatts per square meter.
Panel efficiency is typically 20 percent yielding 280 watts per square meter.
A one megawatt data center requires roughly 3600 square meters of solar panels.
Eclipse periods in LEO last up to 36 minutes per 90-minute orbit.
Battery storage must bridge eclipse gaps for continuous operation.
Radiators at 300 Kelvin reject approximately 450 watts per square meter.
A one megawatt facility requires 2200 square meters of radiator surface.
Higher temperatures improve heat rejection but reduce electronics reliability.
Space data centers are technically feasible at small scales today.
Economic viability requires launch costs below 200 dollars per kilogram.
Thermal management is the primary engineering constraint at scale.
SpaceX Starship targets 10 to 100 dollars per kilogram fully reusable.
Further research on deployable radiator systems is strongly recommended.
This concludes the Space Data Center Power Budget Analysis report.
