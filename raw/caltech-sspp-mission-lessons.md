# Caltech Space Solar Power Project (SSPP) — First In-Space Mission: Successes and Lessons

**Source**: Caltech News
**URL**: https://www.caltech.edu/about/news/space-solar-power-project-ends-first-in-space-mission-with-successes-and-lessons
**Mission dates**: January 3 – November 11, 2023

## DOLCE Deployable Structure

**Successful outcome**: Full deployment achieved despite two anomalies

**Anomaly 1 — Wire-snagging**:
During the intended 3–4 day deployment, a diagonal boom wire became snagged, halting progress and damaging a boom-to-structure connection. Team used onboard cameras and a full-scale lab model to diagnose. Resolution: warming the structure via direct sunlight and Earth-reflected solar energy aided completion.

**Anomaly 2 — Secondary jamming**:
After boom deployment, part of the structure jammed under the deployment mechanism — an anomaly never observed in laboratory tests. Reproduced in lab and resolved by vibrating the entire structure through actuator movements.

**Key quote** (Sergio Pellegrino): "The space test has demonstrated the robustness of the basic concept, which has allowed us to achieve a successful deployment in spite of two anomalies" — and highlighted new insights about ultralight deployable structure design.

## ALBA Solar Cell Testing

- Over 240 days of operational data
- Three entirely new ultralight research-grade solar cell classes tested — never previously flown
- **Current commercial space solar cells cost approximately 100× more than terrestrial equivalents**
- Low-cost gallium arsenide cells performed consistently well
- Perovskite cells showed tremendous variability

## MAPLE Wireless Power Transmission

- Successfully demonstrated wireless space power beaming (announced June 2023)
- Continued testing for eight months beyond initial demonstrations
- Late-mission stress testing revealed reduced transmitted power, attributed to individual transmitter element degradation and thermal-electrical interactions

## Significance

First flight demonstration of a large-area ultralight deployable structure for space solar power. Both deployment anomalies were failure modes not seen in ground testing, confirming that orbital deployment does not extrapolate linearly from laboratory results.
