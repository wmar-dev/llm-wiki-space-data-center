# Orbital Datacenter Startup Economics

**Source**: The Register
**URL**: https://www.theregister.com/2026/04/15/orbital_space_datacenter_startup_economics/
**Published**: April 15, 2026

## Company: Orbital

**CEO**: Euwyn Poon (former leader of electric scooter company Spin)
**Funding**: Investment from a16z speedrun program

## Economics

- Current launch cost: ~$7,000/kg
- Target required: ~$10/kg ("a couple orders of magnitude" reduction)
- CEO assessment: "The constellation approach to run AI inference makes sense, but as of today, the economics of launch don't quite work yet"

## Satellite Specifications

- Size: ~four refrigerators
- Power: 100 kW per satellite
- Poon: 100 kW is "really the sweet spot" — larger power levels make solar and radiator dimensions unwieldy
- Solar array: ~two halves of a tennis court
- Radiator: ~half that area
- Planned chip: Nvidia Space-1 Vera Rubin module
- First proof-of-concept flight: planned for 2027
- Full-scale satellite target: 2030

## Industry Skepticism

- Gartner analyst Bill Ray: described orbital datacenters as "peak insanity," asserting space-based systems won't serve Earth applications "for decades, if ever"

## Significance

Real-world CEO admission that launch economics are not yet viable. The 100 kW "sweet spot" claim is the most specific published figure on practical orbital data center power scale, and directly constrains how the economics can scale.
