## Content

On January 14, 2015 at 3:58 AM CST, ISS telemetry indicated a possible leak of ammonia within Node 2 in the US Segment. The crew performed emergency procedures and subsequently evacuated the US Segment and isolated themselves to the Russian Segment.

The incident involved: **External Thermal Control System (ETCS) Loop B, Node 2 Low Temperature Loop**.

Incident later confirmed to be a false alarm. No actual ammonia release occurred; the anomaly was attributed to sensor contamination (METOX canister regeneration off-gassing is a known false-positive trigger for ISS ammonia sensors).

## Key System Details Referenced

- ETCS Loop B: Port-side external ammonia loop (services P1 truss radiator wing)
- Node 2 Low Temperature Loop: ITCS water sub-loop serving Harmony module at 17–24°C
- Emergency protocol: Full US Segment evacuation to Russian Segment; hatch closure isolation
- Ammonia: Working fluid in all ISS external thermal loops (freeze point −77°C)

## Historical Context

The January 14, 2015 event is one of several ammonia-related false alarms in ISS operational history. A real ETCS ammonia leak into the pressurized volume would be a crew medical emergency (ammonia is highly toxic) and a significant mission risk event requiring emergency evacuation.
