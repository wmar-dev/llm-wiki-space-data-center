# iROSA — Integrated Roll-Out Solar Arrays (NASA)

**Source**: NASA STMD Impact Story
**URL**: https://www.nasa.gov/directorates/stmd/impact-story-roll-out-solar-arrays/

## Key Facts

**Per-panel power output**:
- Each iROSA produces in excess of 28 kW at beginning of life
- ROSA technology capable of more than 30 kW per panel

**ISS installation**:
- Six iROSAs installed total (first two completed June 20, 2021)
- Total ISS power after installation: more than 250 kW (>30% increase over prior capacity)
- Legacy arrays continue producing ~95 kW combined
- Two additional iROSAs requested in 2023 would add ~15 kW

**Technology**:
- Autonomous self-deployment using composite boom strain energy — no motors required
- Rolls compactly for launch; scales from small satellites to deep-space missions
- Composite booms provide structural rigidity and micrometeoroid protection
- Each iROSA is nearly half the size of the legacy ISS arrays

**Other applications**:
- Gateway Power and Propulsion Element: 60 kW from two ROSAs
- Double Asteroid Redirection Test (DART)
- Commercial geostationary satellites

## Significance

iROSA represents the current state of the art in large-area deployable solar arrays. 250 kW total for the entire ISS illustrates the scale gap versus MW-class data centers.
