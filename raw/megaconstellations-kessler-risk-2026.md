# Managing Mega-Constellation Risks in LEO

Source: Viasat white paper (2022, updated 2025)
Type: Industry analysis
Date retrieved: 2026-06-07

Key findings:
- One-third of a single mega-constellation accounts for ~90% of close approaches between two satellites
- 1% failure rate on tens of thousands of satellites yields hundreds of non-maneuverable failed satellites
- Crowding tens of thousands of satellites into LEO generates hundreds of millions of conjunction events over license terms
- Residual collision probability after attempted maneuvers is a significant Kessler Syndrome contributor at scale

Relevance: Orbital data centers (100+ tons, large cross-section) represent disproportionately high collision risk per object compared to Starlink-class satellites.
