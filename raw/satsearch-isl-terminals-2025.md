## Key Context

Optical ISL deployment has been "varied" due to demanding pointing requirements and need for efficient optical ground station capabilities. RF spectrum overcrowding in LEO is driving adoption of laser comms.

**Advantages of optical/laser over RF:**
- No ITU/FCC frequency coordination required
- Higher data rates
- Low probability of intercept/detection (LPI/LPD) — defense relevant
- Lower interference

## Key Observations
- Most commercial products do not publish data rates publicly on the overview page
- Voyager µLCT claims 400+ Gbps — highest commercial claim seen
- CACI CICADA represents low-end commercial: 150 Mbps at 2,000 km
- Tesat (Deutsche Telekom subsidiary) is the heritage ISL vendor (used on Starlink)
- Mynaric CONDOR Mk3 is designed for large LEO constellations (Telesat Lightspeed)
- No products list sustained ground downlink throughput with contact time constraints
