# Unseen Cost of Space Computing: Quantifying LEO Battery Aging via Physics-Driven Modeling

**Authors**: Li Zeng, Jingyang Zhu, Zixin Wang, Yuanming Shi, Khaled B. Letaief
**Source**: arxiv:2603.04372
**Submitted**: March 4, 2026
**URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04372

## Abstract

Addresses how computational demands in Low Earth Orbit satellite constellations accelerate battery degradation through repeated charge-discharge cycles, threatening operational longevity of Space Computing Power Networks (SCPN). Develops a high-fidelity, physics-driven model that quantitatively links computational workload parameters to nonlinear battery degradation.

## Key Claims

- Intensive computation within SCPN incurs a significant "unseen cost" through accelerated physical battery degradation
- Existing methods relying on Depth of Discharge (DoD) metrics overlook the complex, nonlinear nature of actual battery aging processes
- The paper establishes a physics-driven model that directly links execution of a computational task to physical degradation of onboard battery — claimed to be the first such model
- Simulations show: under abundant solar conditions, prioritizing immediate solar energy utilization extends battery life; energy-scarce scenarios benefit from reactive policies monitoring real-time battery state

## Significance

First quantified model linking compute workload scheduling to physical battery degradation in LEO satellites. Directly relevant to orbital data center power management.
