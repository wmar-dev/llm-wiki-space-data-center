# Data Centers in Space

**Source**: Scientific American
**URL**: https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/data-centers-in-space/

## Key Companies and Projects

- **Google Suncatcher**: Plans to launch solar-powered satellite constellations with specialty AI chips; demonstration mission scheduled for 2027; targets <$200/kg launch cost by 2035 for economic viability
- **Starcloud**: Launched a 60 kg satellite with NVIDIA H100 GPU; projects a 5 GW orbital data center by 2035
- **China**: Operating Xingshidai "space data center" constellation
- **EU ASCEND project**: Studying similar concepts

## Technical Requirements

- Launch costs must fall to under $200/kg by 2035 for Google's vision to be viable
- Onboard chips require replacement every 5–6 years (radiation and degradation)
- Sun-synchronous orbit positioning enables nearly constant solar panel exposure

## Performance Claims

- Starcloud estimates 10× lower carbon emissions vs. land-based facilities powered by natural gas generators
- Continuous solar energy availability without cloud or nighttime interruption

## Environmental Concerns

- Saarland University "Dirty Bits in Low-Earth Orbit" paper: orbital solar-powered data centers could generate "an order of magnitude greater emissions than a data center on Earth" — from rocket launches and atmospheric reentry of spacecraft components, including ozone-depleting pollutants
- Potential interference with asteroid detection during twilight observations
- Contribution to space debris accumulation

## Significance

Provides the broadest industry survey. The Saarland University environmental finding is a significant counter-claim to the carbon efficiency narrative, and should be treated as a contested claim requiring further investigation.
