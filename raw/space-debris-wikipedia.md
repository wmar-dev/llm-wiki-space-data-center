# Space debris

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_debris
Fetched: 2026-06-07

Space debris (space junk) are defunct human-made objects in Earth orbit.

Key data points:
- As of April 2025, ESA reports 40,230 tracked artificial objects in orbit
- As of Jan 2019: 128M pieces <1 cm, 900K pieces 1-10 cm, 34,000 pieces >10 cm
- As of June 2023: at least 37,000 pieces >10 cm
- 8,000 metric tons of debris in orbit as of 2020
- Two bands of LEO already past critical density: 900-1000 km and 1500 km
- 85% pollution in LEO
- Average impact speed in LEO: 10 km/s, max >14 km/s
- 2009 satellite collision: closing speed 11.7 km/s, created 2,000+ large fragments
- 2011 US National Research Council: debris at critical level, "tipping point"
- 2006 NASA model: even with no new launches, environment would self-generate by ~2055
- Whipple shielding on ISS, maneuvers for collision risk >1/10,000
- ITU now requires end-of-life disposal plans
- Space debris is a negative externality
- NASA Orbital Debris Program founded 1979
